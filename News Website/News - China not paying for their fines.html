After VFA, gov’t should demand payment from China for ruins in West Philippine Sea —analyst

VIRGIL LOPEZ,GMA News | February 22, 2021 | 11:13PM
West Philippine Sea

The Philippines should also consider demanding payment from China for the destruction of marine resources in the West Philippine Sea, a defense analyst said Monday.

Chester Cabalza, a senior lecturer at the University of the Philippines’ anthropology department, issued the call after President Rodrigo Duterte recently asked the United States to pay in exchange for the continued presence of American troops in the Philippines.

“If we will ask for payment from the US for their security activities in our country and in the region, then we should also ask the same thing from China [for] destroying our maritime resources. It should not be a double standard,” Cabalza said during the Tapatan sa Aristocrat virtual forum.

China has turned several former reefs into artificial islands with military facilities, runways, and surface to air missiles over the years to assert its claim to nearly the entire South China Sea, including areas within the Philippines’ exclusive economic zone.

Last week, opposition Senator Risa Hontiveros said China now owes the Philippines more than P800 billion for the destruction of resources in the areas occupied by Beijing in the West Philippine Sea.

She said other countries like Japan and the US had paid for damages during World War II and the destruction caused by a US Navy ship when it ran aground on Tubbataha Reef, respectively.


Cabalza said he sees nothing wrong with Duterte’s position to ask for payment from the US for the continued implementation of the Visiting Forces Agreement (VFA).


“It’s no longer about cash register diplomacy but it’s about pragmatism because all of us need economic support right now because of the current pandemic,” Cabalza said.


“Bakit naman tayo mahihiyang humingi ng bayad kung talagang kailangan natin? And we will also support them. That’s why we will test our alliance now with the US vis-a-vis China. Magbayad din kayo sa mga sinira ninyo na mga resources namin,” he added.


He said negotiations for compensation should be transparent and should have the concurrence of Congress.


“There must be concurrence from the Congress and the President will only execute it,” Cabalza said.


Ratified by the Senate in 1999, the VFA allows American forces to hold joint military exercises, counter intelligence training and engage in humanitarian aid missions with their Filipino counterparts but prohibits US troops from engaging in combat operations.—LDF, GMA News
